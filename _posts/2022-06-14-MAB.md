# Problem Setting
Generally, the problem in our concern is the *Stochastic Multi-armed Bandits*.
In this setting, the player is presented with a collection of actions, or *arms* to pull each round of play.
Each arm distributes a random reward $$X_{i, t}$$ that follows some subgaussian distribution over $$[0,1]$$, with $$\mathbb{E}[X_{i, t}] = \mu_i$$.
The goal of the player is to minimize the cumulative regret. 
Suppose the player chooses arm $$a_t$$ at round $$t$$, the regret at round $$T$$ could be written as

\begin{equation}
    R(T) = \max\limits_{j} \mathbb{E}[\sum_{t=1}^{T}(X_{j, t}-X_{a_t, t})]
\end{equation}

Another way to write this is to use the reward gap $$\Delta_i = \max_j \mu_j - \mu_i$$.
